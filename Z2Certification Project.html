Brendan Eich

The creator of JavaScript

Here is a brief timeline of his life:

  • 1961: Brendan Eich is born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.
  • 1983: Eich graduates from Santa Clara University with a degree in mathematics and computer science.
  • 1995: Eich is hired by Netscape Communications Corporation to work on the development of a new scripting language for web browsers. He creates JavaScript in just 10 days, drawing heavily on the syntax of other programming languages like C and Java.
  • 1996: Microsoft creates its own version of JavaScript called JScript, which causes some compatibility issues between browsers.
  • 1997: JavaScript is standardized by the European Computer Manufacturers Association (ECMA), becoming known as ECMAScript.
  • 2003: Eich joins the Mozilla Foundation, where he continues to work on the development of JavaScript and the Firefox web browser.
  • 2014: Eich becomes CEO of Mozilla, but resigns just 11 days later following controversy over his support for a political campaign opposed to same-sex marriage.
  • 2017: Eich founds Brave Software, a company that develops a privacy-focused web browser called Brave.

"JavaScript, the 'duct tape' of the Internet, binds together HTML, CSS, and backend services."
